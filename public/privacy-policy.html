1. Who this policy covers

This Privacy Policy explains how NewsFlow ("we", "us", "the app") collects, uses, stores, and shares information from readers, citizen journalists, and administrators who use the NewsFlow app and website (newsflowshots.web.app), operated by [YOUR NAME / BUSINESS NAME], based in [YOUR CITY, STATE, INDIA].

2. Information we collect

2.1 Account information (all users)

DataWhy we collect it
NameDisplayed on your profile and on reports you publish (unless you choose to post anonymously)
Email addressAccount login and identification
Phone numberAccount contact, verification
PasswordStored securely (hashed) via Firebase Authentication — we never see or store your plain-text password
Preferred language (Telugu/English)To show the app in your preferred language
DistrictTo show you local news first, and (for journalists) to restrict reporting to your own registered district

2.2 Additional information (journalists only)

To register as a citizen journalist, we additionally collect:

DataWhy we collect it
Full legal nameEditorial accountability for published reports
Village/town, Mandal, Assembly ConstituencyTo verify your local reporting area and route reports correctly
Political party affiliation (if any)Disclosed to admins only, to help maintain balanced, transparent reporting — never shown to readers
Profile photo (optional)Displayed on your public journalist profile

Political affiliation is sensitive personal data. We collect it only with your explicit consent during registration, we do not publish it, and it is visible only to app administrators for internal editorial-fairness purposes.

2.3 Content you submit

  • Text reports, headlines, and summaries you write or that our AI assistant generates from your notes
  • Photos, videos (up to 90 seconds), and voice recordings you upload with a report
  • Comments and replies you post on articles
  • Likes, dislikes, and bookmarks on articles

2.4 Local advertisement leads (journalists)

If you submit a business lead for potential advertising (e.g. a shop opening, an event, an obituary notice), we collect the business/contact name and phone number you provide us. This may be a third party who has not created an account with NewsFlow. We use this information solely to allow our marketing team to follow up about advertising — we do not use it for any other purpose, and we do not sell or share it outside NewsFlow.

2.5 Wallet and earnings information

  • NewsFlow points earned for approved reports (10 points per approved own-district report)
  • Advertising commission earnings, where applicable
  • Withdrawal requests you submit (amount requested)

Withdrawal payouts are currently processed manually via bank transfer or UPI outside the app. We do not store your bank account or UPI ID inside the app database — payment details are exchanged directly with our team when a withdrawal is approved.

2.6 Push notification token

If you allow notifications, we store a device-specific messaging token (via Firebase Cloud Messaging) so we can send you news alerts. You can disable this at any time in your device's notification settings — this stops new notifications but does not delete previously collected data automatically; contact us to request deletion.

2.7 Automatically collected information

  • App usage data (which articles you view, for view counts)
  • Your selected district and language preference, stored locally on your device (localStorage) so your choice persists between visits

3. How we use your information

  • To operate the news feed, journalist submission, and admin review workflow
  • To personalize your feed by district/language
  • To pay journalists their earned wallet points and advertising commissions
  • To send push notifications about new stories and local advertisements
  • To moderate content and enforce our journalist guidelines (originality, accuracy, sensitivity, and ethics) — including restricting accounts that violate these rules
  • To improve the app and diagnose technical issues

4. Third-party services we use

NewsFlow is built on the following third-party infrastructure, each of which processes data on our behalf:

ServicePurpose
Google Firebase (Authentication, Firestore, Storage, Cloud Messaging, Hosting)Account management, database, file storage, push notifications, website hosting
GroqCloud (AI language models)Converting journalist voice notes to text and generating article drafts from raw notes — your submitted report text/audio is sent to Groq's API for this processing
NewsData.io / public RSS feedsSource of some aggregated news content (not related to your personal data)

These providers may process data outside India. We select providers that maintain industry-standard security practices, but we encourage you to review Google Firebase's privacy policy and Groq's privacy policy as well.

5. Data sharing

We do not sell your personal data. We share data only:

  • With the third-party service providers listed above, strictly to operate the app
  • Publicly, for content you choose to publish (your report, your name if you opt to show it, your profile photo)
  • If required by law, regulation, or a valid legal request from Indian authorities

6. Data retention

We retain your account data for as long as your account is active. Published news articles remain publicly accessible (or hidden by an admin) as part of the public record, even if you later delete your account, unless you specifically request removal. Draft reports awaiting approval are retained for review purposes.

7. Your rights

  • Access: You can view your profile information within the app at any time.
  • Correction: You can update your name, phone, language, district, and profile photo directly in the app.
  • Deletion: You may request deletion of your account and associated personal data by contacting us at the email below. We will process such requests in line with the DPDP Act, 2023, subject to reasonable exceptions (e.g. records we must retain for legal or financial compliance).
  • Withdraw consent: You may disable notifications at any time, and journalists may request removal of political affiliation data by contacting us.

8. Children's privacy

NewsFlow is not directed at children under 18. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal data, please contact us so we can remove it.

9. Security

We use Firebase's security infrastructure, including authentication, encrypted data transmission (HTTPS), and access-control rules that restrict who can read or write different types of data (e.g. only admins can view journalists' political affiliation or approve withdrawal requests). No system is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security of information transmitted to the app.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by updating the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. Continued use of NewsFlow after changes constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
